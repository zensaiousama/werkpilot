# SOP: Client Onboarding Process

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-02-13
**Owner:** Sales & Operations Team
**Last Reviewed:** 2026-02-13

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the standard process for onboarding new clients at Werkpilot. A smooth onboarding experience sets the foundation for a successful long-term partnership and ensures clients quickly realize value from our AI automation services.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all team members involved in client onboarding, including:
- Sales team (handoff)
- Project managers
- Technical setup team
- Content/service delivery team
- Account managers

Covers all Werkpilot service packages: AI Content Automation, Marketing Automation, Operations Automation.

## 3. Prerequisites

Before starting client onboarding:
- [ ] Signed contract and payment terms confirmed
- [ ] Client contact details collected (primary contact, billing contact)
- [ ] Service scope and deliverables clearly defined in the proposal
- [ ] Internal project team assigned
- [ ] Airtable client record created
- [ ] Welcome email template ready

## 4. Step-by-Step Procedure

### Phase 1: Pre-Onboarding (Day -2 to Day 0)

#### Step 1: Sales Handoff (Sales -> PM)
1. Sales creates the handoff document including:
   - Client background and industry
   - Pain points and goals
   - Agreed scope, timeline, and budget
   - Key stakeholders and decision-makers
   - Any specific requirements or sensitivities
2. Sales introduces PM to client via email
3. PM reviews all proposal documents and contract terms

#### Step 2: Internal Setup
1. Create client folder in Google Drive / Notion
2. Set up client in Airtable (Clients table)
3. Create project in project management tool
4. Assign team members to the project
5. Set up communication channel (Slack channel or email thread)
6. Prepare onboarding kit documents

### Phase 2: Welcome & Discovery (Day 1-3)

#### Step 3: Welcome Communication
1. Send personalized welcome email including:
   - Thank you and excitement about the partnership
   - Introduction to the project team
   - Overview of the onboarding timeline
   - Link to the onboarding questionnaire
   - Next steps and first meeting invitation
2. Include the Werkpilot Client Handbook (PDF)

#### Step 4: Kick-off Meeting (60 min)
Agenda:
1. Introductions (10 min)
2. Confirm goals and success metrics (15 min)
3. Review scope and timeline (10 min)
4. Communication preferences and cadence (10 min)
5. Access and tools setup requirements (10 min)
6. Q&A (5 min)

Document all decisions and action items in meeting notes.

#### Step 5: Discovery & Data Collection
1. Send onboarding questionnaire (if not yet completed)
2. Collect:
   - Brand guidelines and style guides
   - Existing content/assets
   - Access credentials (CMS, social media, analytics)
   - Target audience documentation
   - Competitor information
   - Existing workflows to automate
3. Schedule technical setup call if needed

### Phase 3: Setup & Configuration (Day 3-7)

#### Step 6: Technical Setup
1. Configure AI agents for the client's specific needs
2. Set up integrations (CMS, email, social media, CRM)
3. Create client-specific prompt templates
4. Configure quality assurance workflows
5. Set up reporting dashboards
6. Test all automations in sandbox mode

#### Step 7: Content/Service Calibration
1. Produce 2-3 sample deliverables
2. Share with client for feedback
3. Iterate on tone, style, and quality expectations
4. Document calibrated standards in the client profile
5. Update prompt templates based on feedback

### Phase 4: Go-Live & Stabilization (Day 7-14)

#### Step 8: Soft Launch
1. Begin delivering at 50% capacity
2. Daily quality checks for the first week
3. Collect client feedback after each deliverable batch
4. Address any issues immediately

#### Step 9: Full Launch
1. Ramp up to full delivery capacity
2. Establish regular review cadence (weekly/biweekly)
3. Set up automated reporting

#### Step 10: Onboarding Completion
1. Conduct 2-week review call with client
2. Confirm satisfaction and address any concerns
3. Document lessons learned
4. Officially mark onboarding as "completed" in Airtable
5. Transition to ongoing account management cadence

## 5. Quality Checkpoints

| Checkpoint | When | Responsible | Criteria |
|-----------|------|-------------|----------|
| Handoff complete | Day 0 | Sales + PM | All docs transferred, intro made |
| Kick-off done | Day 1-2 | PM | Goals, scope, timeline confirmed |
| Technical setup | Day 5 | Tech team | All integrations working |
| Calibration approved | Day 7 | Client + PM | Sample deliverables approved |
| Soft launch stable | Day 10 | QA team | No critical issues |
| Onboarding complete | Day 14 | PM + Client | Client confirms satisfaction |

## 6. Escalation Paths

- **Client unresponsive:** Follow up after 24h, escalate to sales contact after 48h
- **Technical integration issues:** Escalate to IT lead within 4 hours
- **Scope disagreement:** Escalate to sales lead and review contract
- **Quality concerns from client:** Immediate escalation to quality lead + PM
- **Budget/payment issues:** Escalate to finance team

## 7. Common Mistakes

- Rushing through discovery; not understanding the client's real needs
- Not confirming communication preferences early
- Skipping the calibration phase and going straight to full delivery
- Not documenting decisions from meetings
- Promising deliverables outside the agreed scope
- Not setting clear expectations about response times
- Forgetting to introduce all relevant team members
